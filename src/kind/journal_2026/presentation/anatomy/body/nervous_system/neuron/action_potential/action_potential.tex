\begin{frame}[allowframebreaks]{Action potential(Spike)}
    When a receptor (sensor) is provoked by a stimulus, then a voltage difference between the nucleus and axon terminals is created which is called \textbf{action potential} \cite{hodgkin-1952-a-quantitative-description-of-membrane-current-and-its-application-to-conduction-and-excitation-in-nerve}
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Action potential has an oscillatory behavior. That is, the volatage goes first high and then goes lower than zero and then returns back to zero
    \end{itemize}
